Course Format

By the end of the course, students should have:
  • Each week there is 1 Lecture Day and 1 Discussion Day
  • Weekly or Biweekly Homework Assignments (individual and team-based)
  • Assigned Readings
  • Final Project (individual or team-based)
  • Final Course Document (individual)

Readings

There will be a set of assigned readings either from primary reference materials or from prepared course materials. Homework will test reading comprehension, critical thinking, and the ability to connect and associate different ideas and authors. In Springâ€™24, we will be using Perusal.

Each student will have the opportunity to act as a discussion leader. A discussion leader will lead the discussion of the readings of that week. Student discussion leaders will be assigned on a rotating basis and a schedule will be determined in the beginning of the semester.

Homework

This course will involve homework assignments most of which will be hand written exercises. Earlier in the semester, assignments will also involve short, one or 2-week, projects that will require oral presentation in class for peer feedback and critique. Later, after about 2/3 of the semester, students will work individually or in teams of two on a larger final project.

Final Course Document

An important aspect in any designer's (or creator's) work is the concise but visually appealing and informative work documentation. Every student will submit at the end of the semester a digital documentation of their work in this class, including homework assignments, projects, and writings. The deadline for this document is the last day of Spring term examinations (see grading below).

Materials and Access

All course materials and assignments including excerpts from readings will be provided through Canvas. There is no standard textbook for this course but there is an extensive readings list (both mandatory and optional).
